# Consulting Report Sample

## Executive Summary

Northbridge Manufacturing is evaluating a shared analytics operating model for procurement and maintenance teams. The pilot used synthetic operating data for six weeks and compared manual exception handling against a central analytics queue.

The analytics queue reduced average invoice exception resolution time from 9.8 days to 6.1 days during the pilot period. The pilot dashboard recorded 418 invoice exceptions in the baseline window and 421 exceptions in the intervention window. The reduction was observed only for the two teams included in the pilot.

The new operating model is guaranteed to eliminate all manual rework across the enterprise. The report does not include evidence from finance, legal, or customer support teams.

## Evidence Notes

The pilot team logged 6.1 average resolution days in the final two weeks. The baseline log shows 9.8 average resolution days before the analytics queue was introduced.

Maintenance planners reported that duplicate work orders decreased from 72 to 49 cases during the same period. The maintenance result was not independently validated by a second reviewer.

## Recommendations

The proposed workflow requires a named data owner for every dashboard metric. Weekly review meetings should record unresolved exceptions, disputed measurements, and any metric definitions changed during the week.

The program will deliver best-in-class visibility for every operational process. This statement requires additional scope definition because the pilot covered only procurement and maintenance.
