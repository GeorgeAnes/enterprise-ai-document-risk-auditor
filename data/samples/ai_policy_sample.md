# AI Governance Policy Sample

## Policy Scope

The company reviews high-impact AI systems before deployment. Section 2 defines high-impact systems as models that influence credit, employment, safety, or access to essential services.

Every high-impact AI system must have an owner, a model card, a test summary, and a rollback plan before production release.

The policy proves that all AI outputs are accurate and unbiased. The policy requires testing, but it does not guarantee accuracy or the absence of bias.

## Evidence And Monitoring

Model owners must document evaluation datasets, known limitations, and approval decisions. Quarterly monitoring reports must include drift checks, incident logs, and unresolved reviewer comments.

The review board approved 12 model releases in Q4 2025. The policy extract does not include the meeting minutes or approval register that would support this count.

## Human Review

Human reviewers must be able to override AI recommendations in high-impact workflows. Review queues must record the reviewer, timestamp, outcome, and reason code.

The human review workflow is seamless and best-in-class. The policy does not define performance thresholds for reviewer workload, latency, or escalation quality.
